# Theory

## The Core Idea

If spacetime has a compact extra dimension with radius R ≈ 1 cm, the first Kaluza-Klein excitation frequency is f₁ = c/(2πR) ≈ 4.77 GHz. Instead of building a TeV collider to probe this, we build a copper cavity tuned to resonate at exactly that frequency.

## Why Geometry, Not Energy?

The Casimir effect proves that quantum phenomena can be accessed through geometry (plate separation) rather than energy. Josephson junctions measure fundamental constants at millivolt scales. We propose the same principle applies to extra dimensions: a standing wave whose nodal structure matches the KK compactification circle can couple energy into the bulk.

## The Coupling Equation

```
κ = ε² × Q × F × Γ(θ)
```

- **ε ≈ 10⁻³**: gravitational-electromagnetic mixing parameter
- **Q > 1000**: cavity quality factor (room-temperature copper)
- **F = 0.77**: Megidish temporal fidelity
- **Γ(θ)**: geometric overlap integral (0 to 1)

Maximum coupling κ_max ≈ 0.77 gives signal power ~-42 dBm, SNR ~33 dB above HackRF noise floor.

## The Harmony Condition

The system couples to the bulk when standing-wave nodes × wavelength = KK circumference:

```
N × λ_cav = 2πR
```

The harmony operators Ô_n = exp(2πin/N) form cyclic group Z_N. For N=11, this connects to base-11 number theory.

## Temporal Entanglement Interpretation

Megidish et al. (2013) entangled photons that never coexisted in time. We interpret this as the temporal gap being bridged through the compact dimension. The fidelity F = 0.77 is the bulk transmission coefficient — analogous to Fresnel coefficients at a dielectric interface, but for the 4D-5D boundary.

## CPT Connection

The three CPT symmetries correspond to three 120° rotations on the KK circle. CPT = 1 is a topological winding constraint. The Euler-Mascheroni constant γ = 0.5772... measures the accumulated phase mismatch per cycle — the price of maintaining CPT = 1 in discretized spacetime.
